Welcome to the online home of Frank Passaro!

Frank Passaro has been a Maryland resident since 1992. He grew up in East Towson and graduated from the Immaculate Heart of Mary School in 1967. He returned to the parish after a fifteen year period during which he and is family lived in Western Maryland and Kansas. Frank is a graduate of Calvert Hall High School and Georgetown University. He holds a Master’s Degree in Education from Towson University. Frank has been a teacher and administrator in public and parochial schools in Maryland and Kansas over the past thirty years. He sings in the IHM Contemporary Ensemble, has worked in children’s ministry, acts as a Eucharistic minister, and, with his wife, Peggy, acts as a marriage preparation counselor for people being wed at the church. Frank is proud to be a professed member of the Secular Franciscan Order.

Franks parents, the late Frank Passaro and his widow, Simona, were members of the IHM community since April, 1953. Frank’s wife Peggy, an IHM and Mercy High graduate, works as an administrative secretary for the Baltimore County Public Schools. Their two sons, Mike and Pat, have also been active in the Church. Mike, 25, is a graduate of Towson State University and holds a degree in law from the University of Baltimore School of Law. Pat, 19, is an honor student in Business Administration, attending Neumann College, a Franciscan school of higher learning.

Frank is currently leading the Social Studies Department at Calvert Hall. He teaches World History to freshman students, as well as a sideline in Italian at the Rev. Oreste Pandola Adult Learning Center in Little Italy, Baltimore Maryland.

Frank acts as an educational consultant for the Advanced Placement® program in World History. He has consulted with schools and school districts in Maryland and beyond. His work as also carried him to Italy.
